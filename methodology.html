Methods overview

Six pillars of the experiment

1. Two arms, one synthetic dossier

Both arms draft against the same fictional GC-301 Phase 2b psoriasis study under ICH E6(R3). Arm A is traditional protocol authoring; Arm B operationalizes E6(R3)’s Critical-to-Quality (CtQ) factor register inside the protocol body, with companion documents (CtQ register, risk-tier table, decision tree D7).

2. The 100-scenario battery

Layer A: 7 hand-authored scenarios derived from Gourav’s 15-year operational experience. Layer B: 42 LLM-generated scenarios seeded on the post-2024 RBM, DCT, and amendment-driver literature. Layer C: 51 scenarios mined directly from peer-reviewed and regulator-published case studies. All 100 are scored against each arm.

3. Six pre-registered metrics

M1 amendments triggered; M2 queries per CRF page; M3 patient-impacting deviations per 100 patients; M4 amendment cost (USD); M5 need-to-amend cycle (days); M6 CtQ rubric quality (0–32, blinded triplicate scoring).

4. Blinded triplicate rubric scoring

32-cell rubric: 8 CtQ families × 4 score levels (Absent / Mentioned / Defined / Operationalized). Three independent blinded reviewers; inter-rater agreement reported; ties resolved by adjudication.

5. Cost model

Anchored on Getz / Tufts CSDD 2024 (Phase 3 amendment cost $535K, Phase 2b derate $320K), 22-site network at $85K/week per-site burn, and a 475-day full amendment cycle (215 days need-to-amend + 260 days implementation-to-approval).

6. Pre-registration + open data

Protocol, scenario battery, rubric, scoring procedure, and simulator harness are pre-registered before Phase 4 simulation. All inputs and outputs ship on the public repository under a permissive license.
